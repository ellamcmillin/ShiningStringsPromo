WAWM Shining Strings

Who are we?
The WAWM Shining Strings is an advanced string group that plays gigs and concerts around the West Allis and Milwaukee area. The ages of the musicians in this orchestra range from 6th grade in middle school to 12th grade in high school. In the last Shining Strings season, in 2016/2017, we played for the Tripoli Shrine Ladies' Luncheon. We also played for the residents at the Village at Manor Park.


What kind of music/instruments do we play?
We play all kinds of music from pop, to classical, to rock. We even sometimes play music from your faveorite movies. A shining Strings classic is Lady Gaga Hit Mix arranged by Larry Moore. We play that piece at every gig we do. You'd think we would be sick of it but we aren't, we love to play that piece! The instruments we play are as follows:

Violin 1 (melody) & Volin 2 harmony/backup)

Viola

Cello

Bass

How Do I Get Involved?
To get invoved with Shining strings, you can talk to the director of the orchestra, Mrs. Mary Pat Michels. Or if you know someone in the orchestra, then you talk about them giving her your name. The director will then make you a binder of all the music for that season.
